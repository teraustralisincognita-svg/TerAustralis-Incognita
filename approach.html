Approach

Doing it properly, from the very beginning.

Ambitious projects fail when they cut corners early and pay for it later. TerAustralis Incognita is structured to get the order right — principles first, partnerships first, compliance from day one.

Coordination & advocacy layer

We sit between the players who already exist — miners, processors, manufacturers, researchers, agencies and communities — and help them move in the same direction. We make the case publicly and in policy, and we convene the conversations that wouldn't otherwise happen. We don't compete with the industry we're trying to build.

Onshore focus

Recovery, refining and processing happen in Australia. That's where the strategic value lives, and it's the difference between exporting rocks and holding capability. Reclaiming minerals from existing tailings and waste is a priority — getting more from what's already disturbed before disturbing more.

Phased compliance

Environmental, regulatory and safety obligations are designed in from the start and met in deliberate stages — not bolted on under pressure. Doing it in the right order is slower at the beginning and far more durable in the end.

Genuine partnership — FPIC

We work to the standard of Free, Prior and Informed Consent with Traditional Owners and affected communities. That means consent, not just consultation; involvement before decisions are made, not after; and a real share in the benefits — not a signature at the end of a process.

Relationship with Second Founding

Part of a larger renewal.

TerAustralis Incognita does not stand alone. It is aligned with Second Founding — a broader effort to renew the Australian project on honest terms, beginning with truth, consent and a shared sense of what this country can be.

Where Second Founding holds the wider national conversation, TerAustralis Incognita is its industrial and space expression: a concrete, buildable example of what renewal looks like when it's pointed at something tangible. The same principles run through both — they simply express them at different scales.

We would rather earn trust than spend it, and build something that lasts rather than something that merely launches. Doing it properly from the beginning isn't a constraint on the ambition — it's what makes the ambition credible.
