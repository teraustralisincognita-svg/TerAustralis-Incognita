The value chain

Why minerals are the foundation of space.

Satellites, sensors, magnets, propulsion and power all begin as ore in the ground. Sovereign space capability is impossible without sovereign control of the materials beneath it.

  1. 01

    Onshore recovery

    Recover and reclaim critical minerals and rare earths within Australia — including from tailings and waste streams others have written off.

  2. 02

    Refine & process

    Build onshore refining and processing so value, jobs and capability stay sovereign rather than being shipped out as raw rock.

  3. 03

    Space infrastructure

    Feed processed materials into the satellites, launch hardware and multiplanetary infrastructure of an independent Australian space industry.

Why now

The window to do this properly is open — briefly.

Demand for critical minerals is climbing, supply chains are being redrawn, and space is becoming national infrastructure. Australia has the resources and the talent. What it has lacked is coordination — and a commitment to building the foundation right.

See the momentum Read the full vision →

  • Surging demand. Rare earths and critical minerals underpin everything from satellites to defence — and the world is short of refined supply.

  • A sovereignty gap. Raw exports leave the value, the jobs and the strategic capability somewhere else.

  • A national appetite. Government and industry are actively seeking onshore processing and a credible space pathway.

  • A chance to set the standard. Build it on consent and shared benefit now, and it holds for generations.

The vision

Red dust to rockets — where it gets built.

Three strategic pillars turn the value chain into places on the map: a Red Centre analog base, a Pilbara recovery hub, and alignment with national renewal.

Read the full vision →
Pillar 01

Marsbase Down Under

A Red Centre analog and training ground — crewed missions, ISRU and rover logistics in true desert conditions, with Mars Society Australia.

 Pillar 02

Pilbara Recovery Hub

Deep-water ports and industry repurposed for reusable-vehicle recovery, refurbishment, manufacturing and propellant production.
Pillar 03

National Renewal

Alignment with the Second Founding of Australia — space-industrial zones and participatory governance with guaranteed Indigenous representation.
