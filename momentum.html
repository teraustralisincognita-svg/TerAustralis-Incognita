Momentum

Real work, honestly reported.

This is an early-stage initiative, and we report it as one. What follows is what has actually been done — no announcements dressed up as achievements, no partners named before they've agreed to be.

Early

Early-stage by design. We are building the foundations before the headlines.

Public

The vision is already stated plainly and consistently in the open, under our own names.

Honest

We would rather under-claim and over-deliver than the other way around.

What we've done so far

The receipts, in plain terms. Specific dates and details can be added here as each item is published or confirmed.

  1. Live

    Public positioning established

    The project now has a clear public voice — stating the vision openly and consistently rather than working in private.

  2. Submitted

    Op-ed written and submitted

    A long-form opinion piece making the public case for sovereign Australian space capability — built on onshore critical minerals and rare earths — has been written and submitted to national publications.

  3. Ongoing

    Direct outreach underway

    Deliberate, individual outreach to people across the space sector, the critical-minerals industry, advanced manufacturing, research and policy — finding the people who already want this and bringing them into the same conversation.

  4. In progress

    Early conversations

    A number of early conversations with potential collaborators and aligned voices are underway. We're keeping these quiet until there is something real and agreed to announce — names get mentioned once people have chosen to stand alongside the project, not before.

  5. Ongoing

    A growing network

    A slowly growing group of people who want a sovereign, onshore, consent-based version of this future — and who are willing to help build it properly rather than quickly.

What's next

The near-term plan.

Concrete, honest next steps — the things we're actually working towards now.

  • Turn conversations into commitments

    Move the strongest early conversations towards collaborators who are ready to be named and counted.

  • Publish a partner brief

    A clear, public one-pager for government, industry and investors that explains the opportunity and the ask.

  • Map the onshore value chain

    Lay out the recovery-to-space pipeline and identify the gaps worth closing first to build real sovereign capability.

  • Formalise the consent framework

    Set out how Free, Prior and Informed Consent and genuine benefit-sharing will work in practice from the start.

A note on honesty

We'll only ever claim what we can stand behind.

Credibility is the whole game for a project like this. So this page will grow with real milestones as they happen, and it will never inflate them. If you want to help turn this momentum into something larger, there's a place for you.
