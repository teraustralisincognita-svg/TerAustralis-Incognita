The Vision

Songlines to Starlines.

A sovereign Australian future that reaches from Country to the stars — the world's oldest living knowledge carried forward with new technology, and built on consent, privacy, and care.

Songlines to Starlines · Red Dust to Rockets · Dreamtime to Deep Space

What we're dreaming

TerAustralis Incognita imagines an independent Australia that meets the space age on its own terms — turning onshore critical minerals and rare earths into real capability, and letting the knowledge that has read this land and sky for tens of thousands of years guide how we step off it. A future where reaching outward deepens our roots, rather than severing them.

It's a long horizon, and we hold it plainly: this is a coordination and advocacy effort in its early days — a place to gather people, ideas and care around a sovereign future, not a launch operator and not a finished thing. The vision is large on purpose. The work underneath it is honest and one step at a time.

The project

TerAustralis Incognita

"The unknown southern land" — the old cartographers' name for a continent imagined before it was charted. We reclaim it for a future still being dreamed into being.

The emblem

The Crystal Dragon & the Longship

A dragon of light arcing across the starlines, and a sovereign longship beneath it — old courage carried toward new sky. The symbols the vision travels under.

A guiding story

The Seven Sisters.

Among the oldest stories carried across this continent and written in the night sky is the songline of the Seven Sisters — an account of pursuit and protection, of women navigating by the stars, of strength held in company. We hold it as a guiding image of resilience, feminine strength, and collective care.

This story belongs to its Traditional Custodians. We carry it with respect and only ever with consent — honouring it, never claiming it, and deferring always to the knowledge-holders to whom it belongs.

The covenant

How we hold all of it.

The vision can be vast because the commitments under it are firm. These aren't features; they're the ground rules.

01

Radical consent

Free, prior and informed consent (FPIC) at the centre. Nothing taken; everything offered and agreed.

02

Indigenous leadership

First Nations knowledge, guidance and benefit are foundational — not decoration, and not an afterthought.

03

Privacy & sovereignty

Data and decisions stay sovereign — close to the people and Country they belong to.

04

Mental autonomy

The mind is inviolable. No technology of ours reaches into a person's inner life without their full, ongoing consent.

05

Cultural care

Sacred knowledge is handled with the respect it's owed, on the terms of those it belongs to.

06

Honest scope

We say plainly what's real, what's in the making, and what's still a dream. No overclaiming.

Where the work stands

Today, this is an early initiative finding its feet: a working reasoning framework in development, a growing body of concept art and writing, and a vision taking shape in the open. The dream is multiplanetary; the footing is honest and present. Those are the foundations — real, finished things — and we let the vision grow from them rather than ahead of them.

A note on this page

This is a vision — and it's written in the language a vision deserves: mythic, hopeful, reaching. The poetic register here expresses direction and meaning, not finished capability. The concrete technology behind the project is described separately and plainly. Both are true; we keep the line between them clear on purpose.
