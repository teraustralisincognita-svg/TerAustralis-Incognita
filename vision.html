Vision

Songlines to Starlines

Over two thousand years ago, the Greek philosopher Aristotle looked at the stars and proposed there must be a great southern land to balance the continents of the north.

Centuries later, this idea appeared on maps as 'Terra Australis Incognita' — the unknown southern land. What was once just a philosopher's vision became our home.

Today, TerAustralis is known — and from this southern land, we now look to the stars to map our future.

Just as First Nations peoples have long mapped the land and skies through Songlines — including the Seven Sisters story of the Pleiades — we weave new Starlines today through song, screen and story, honouring that ancient tradition and the custodians who hold it.

The Western world too has reached for the stars, sending humanity's first intentional hello to the cosmos with the Arecibo message towards the Messier 13 cluster in 1974. From this southern land, we're carrying both ancient and modern stories as we reach for the stars.

What it is

A mission, and the machinery to reach it.

TerAustralis Incognita — "the unknown southern land" — is an initiative to give Australia genuine, sovereign space capability: the ability to build, supply and sustain space infrastructure without depending on others for the things that matter most.

The mission begins underground. The critical minerals and rare earths that make modern technology possible are abundant across this continent, yet most leave as raw ore to be refined and turned into value somewhere else. We exist to help reverse that — to keep recovery, refining and processing onshore, and to point that capability deliberately at space.

This is a long arc, from red dust to rockets. Our role is to connect the parts of it that already exist, advocate for the parts that don't yet, and keep the whole thing anchored to its principles.

Core themes

Three threads, carried forward together.

An integrated, distinctly Australian pathway — one that honours our oldest continuous cultures while forging world-leading space capability.

Red Dust to Rockets

Leverage Australia's iconic red deserts and mineral wealth across the full space value chain — mining, processing, manufacturing, launch support, recovery and refurbishment.

Songlines to Starlines

Extend Aboriginal Songlines to modern orbital paths, interplanetary trajectories and deep-space navigation. Indigenous celestial knowledge — including the Seven Sisters — informs mission design and domain awareness.

Dreamtime to Deep Space

Bridge Dreamtime cosmology and connection to Country with humanity's expansion into space — through ethical, culturally grounded exploration and custodianship.

Starlines

A song of our own.

Songlines are the living knowledge of First Nations peoples — sacred, and held by their custodians. Starlines are not a claim on them. They are a modern tribute: the same human impulse to map meaning onto the stars, carried forward in song, screen and story by the artists and storytellers of today.

An original song written for this vision — a Starline of our own. It echoes the story above: the Arecibo message reaching toward Messier 13, humanity calling out into the great cosmic silence that Fermi's paradox names.

The core: minerals to space

From ore in the ground to infrastructure in orbit.

Every stage that stays onshore is value, capability and security that stays sovereign. This is the pathway TerAustralis Incognita works to join up.

  1. Stage 01

    Recover

    Critical minerals and rare earths recovered onshore — including from existing tailings and waste — reducing new disturbance and reclaiming what's already been dug.

  2. Stage 02

    Refine

    Separation and refining done in Australia, so the high-value, high-skill step isn't handed offshore along with the strategic leverage it carries.

  3. Stage 03

    Manufacture

    Refined materials feeding magnets, batteries, sensors, alloys and components — the building blocks of satellites and launch hardware.

  4. Stage 04

    Reach space

    A sovereign supply chain underwriting Australian satellites, space infrastructure and, in time, multiplanetary capability.

Multiplanetary infrastructure — the hardware that will operate beyond Earth — runs on exactly these materials. A nation that can refine and manufacture them at home is a nation that can participate in that future on its own terms, rather than buying a seat at someone else's table.

Strategic pillars

Three places where this gets built.

From central-desert analog missions to a coastal recovery hub to alignment with national renewal — concrete sites for a multi-planetary Australia.

Pillar 01

Marsbase Down Under — Red Centre Analog & Training Ground

Vast central deserts — the Simpson, Great Victoria and Arkaroola regions — as a proving ground for the demands of life off-world: multi-month crewed missions, habitat testing, in-situ resource utilisation (ISRU), rover logistics and psychological resilience training.

  • Analog missions that model life on Mars — here, on Country.
  • ISRU and rover logistics tested in true desert conditions.
  • In partnership with Mars Society Australia.
Pillar 02

Pilbara Recovery & Refurbishment Hub

Deep-water ports such as Port Hedland and established industrial infrastructure repurposed for reusable-vehicle recovery, refurbishment, manufacturing and propellant production — drawing on local resources, with dual-use potential alongside DARC.

  • Recovery and refurbishment of reusable launch vehicles.
  • Manufacturing and propellant production from local resources.
  • Deep-water port access, with dual-use potential alongside DARC.
Pillar 03

Integration with National Renewal — the Second Founding

Align with the Second Founding of Australia and its vision of 72 new cities — incorporating space-industrial zones and participatory governance with guaranteed Indigenous representation. We are actively collaborating with Second Founding initiatives, following recent discussions, and seek broader partnerships.

  • Yarning circles — men's and women's — as a decision-making model.
  • Recognising the contributions of First Nations peoples, convicts and skilled migrants.
  • The music, screen and entertainment industries as a modern-day expression of Songlines.
  • Building on existing frameworks — keeping what works, removing what doesn't, adding what's needed.
  • Revenue from sovereign minerals and space activities funding broader initiatives.

Our role

A coordination and advocacy layer — not a launch operator.

Clarity matters. We are deliberate about what we do and what we don't, so partners always know exactly what they're working with.

What we are

  • A connective layer joining minerals, manufacturing, policy and community.
  • An advocate making the public and political case for sovereign capability.
  • A convener bringing the right people into the same room, early.
  • A keeper of the principles the work is built on.

What we are not

  • A launch provider or rocket company.
  • A mining company or a single commercial venture.
  • A body that speaks for communities rather than with them.
  • In a hurry to claim more than the work has earned.
